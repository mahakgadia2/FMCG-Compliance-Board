AYUSH Herbal Guidelines — Ministry of AYUSH Guidance on Herbal Ingredients in Consumer Products

Section 1.1 — Scope
These guidelines apply to any consumer product containing herbal/Ayurvedic ingredients above flavoring-threshold quantities, or making traditional-medicine-style claims, regardless of whether the product is also regulated by FSSAI as a food.

Section 1.2 — Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera)
Ashwagandha root extract is a recognized Ayurvedic ingredient. For food/dietary products, the permissible daily dosage is capped at 600mg of standardized root extract per serving for adults. Products marketed to children require a lower cap of 250mg per serving and must carry a caution statement recommending consultation with a healthcare provider before use in children under 12.

Section 1.3 — Permissible Ingredient List
Tulsi (Holy Basil), Turmeric, Ginger, Ashwagandha, Brahmi, and Shatavari are on the AYUSH-recognized permissible ingredient list for wellness/dietary products, subject to dosage caps published in Schedule H of these guidelines.

Section 1.4 — General Caution
Any herbal ingredient not on the permissible list, or used above its dosage cap, requires a NEEDS_REVIEW classification and additional toxicological data before approval.
