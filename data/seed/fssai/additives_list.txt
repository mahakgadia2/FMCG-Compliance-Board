FSSAI Permitted Food Additives — Food Safety and Standards (Food Products Standards and Food Additives) Regulations, 2011

Section 4.1 — General Principle
Only additives listed in Appendix A, within the specified maximum permissible limits for the relevant food category, may be used. Any additive not listed is presumed prohibited for that category.

Section 4.2 — Preservatives
Sodium benzoate is permitted in beverages up to 600mg/kg. Potassium sorbate is permitted in bakery products up to 1000mg/kg. Sulphur dioxide is restricted in products marketed to children under FSSAI's precautionary guidance.

Section 4.3 — Colors
Synthetic colors (e.g. Tartrazine, Sunset Yellow) are restricted to a combined maximum of 100mg/kg in any product and must be declared by name, not just "added color", on products marketed to children.

Section 4.4 — Herbal/Botanical Additives in Food Products
Botanical ingredients used purely for flavoring (e.g. small quantities of ashwagandha root extract as a flavoring agent) are permitted under FSSAI if present below the threshold that would classify the product as having a "medicinal" function; above that threshold, the product falls jointly under AYUSH's purview for ingredient-safety review.

Section 4.5 — Protein/Functional Food Additives
Added protein isolates, fiber, and vitamin/mineral fortificants are permitted under the Food Safety and Standards (Fortification of Foods) Regulations, 2018, subject to the declared fortification logo and standardized claim language ("fortified with").
